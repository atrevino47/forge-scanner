## Core Concepts

### The 3A Framework of Reframing
The single most important skill separating top closers from the rest. Reframing is the 1-3 sentences you say after a prospect says anything but "yes" that increases the likelihood your next statement gets them to buy.

**The 3 A's:**
1. **Acknowledge** — Say back what they said. Shows active listening. Buys you processing time.
2. **Associate** — Connect their question/objection to the behavior of successful customers. "That's actually a question our best clients ask."
3. **Ask** — Ask a question about their question. Attack the frame, not the person. Stay in control.

### The Person Asking Questions Controls the Conversation
"The person asking the questions is the one who's in control." If you're answering, you're on defense. If they're asking, you're losing. Hollywood interrogation rule: "I'm the one asking the questions here."

### Be Like Smoke — You Can't Catch Smoke
Always sidestep. Never be pinned down on a direct answer until you've reframed. You're always shifting, asking questions about questions. No one can disagree with a question.

## Key Techniques

### 1. Never Answer Questions Directly
Instead of answering (which makes them judge/jury/executioner of your answer), ask a question about their question.

**Example:**
- Prospect: "What certifications do your trainers have?"
- BAD: "They have X and Y certifications" (prospect judges if that's good enough)
- GOOD: "Which certifications are you looking for specifically?"
- Most people don't even know what they're asking — they ask because they feel like they should

### 2. The Acknowledge-Associate-Ask Sequence
**Full example:**
- Prospect: "What certifications do your trainers have?"
- **Acknowledge:** "So you're curious about our certifications — great question."
- **Associate:** "It actually shows you're a really rational person making a serious decision, which is amazing."
- **Ask:** "Which certifications are you looking for specifically?"

### 3. Three Types of Association (Straw Men for Tough Truths)
When you need to deliver a hard truth without insulting the prospect:

**Type 1 — Recent conversation:** "It's so funny, I had someone just earlier today ask the same question. Can I share what I shared with them?" (Now you can be brutal about the "other person")

**Type 2 — Successful past customer:** "That makes you just like Sarah, who had a similar business and crushed it with us. She asked this same question..." (Associate them with success)

**Type 3 — Appeal to authority:** "Alex just told me this thing earlier today — can I share it with you?" (You're the messenger, not the authority making the claim)

### 4. Label and Callback
Give prospects a positive label early ("you seem like a really rational person", "you're clearly a good family man"). Later in the close, callback to that label: "Because you're the kind of rational person who [makes good decisions / invests in what works]..."

### 5. Universal Response: "What are you afraid of?"
Hormozi's go-to for any resistance further in the sale:
- "What are you afraid of having happen?"
- Gets to the heart of the issue fastest
- Earlier in sale, softer: "What's your main concern?" → "What are the variables you're considering?"
- Later: "What are you most afraid of?"

### 6. "What would make this a no?"
Keeps you in the sale. Forces them to articulate specific concerns you can address rather than vague resistance.

### 7. "What would it take for you to say yes?"
Flips from defending to collaborative problem-solving.

## Objection Handling Scripts

### "I need to think about it"
- "Totally understand. What are the main things you're considering?"
- "What are the variables in your decision?"
- "What's your main concern?"
- "What would make this a no?"

### "Timing is off / I don't have time"
- "Totally understand. Timing is tough right now — I think it's smart you're already thinking about implementation. Our biggest success stories are actually people who felt the same way. So what would make it a good time?"
- (Never say "it's not about time, it's about priorities" directly — use straw man instead)

### "I need to talk to my spouse"
- "Totally understand — super fair. Just so I understand, what parts do you think they wouldn't like? What would they disagree with?"
- (Brings them back into the sale discussing specifics)

### "I hate this particular feature"
- "Totally get it. I hate them all too — just kidding. Let me ask: why not?"
- (Humor + immediate question to stay in control)

### "Why aren't you answering my questions?"
Two analogies:
- **The Secretary:** "It would be like asking the secretary about how your heart surgery is going — you want the surgeon. We can give much better answers once we start working together."
- **The Mechanic:** "It would be unethical for me to answer without looking under the hood first."
- Then seal it: "And if anyone gives you answers on the first call without already doing an assessment — run the other way."

## Psychological Principles

### 1. Prospects Believe Nothing You Say, Everything They Say
The goal isn't to tell them they're a good fit. It's to ask questions so THEY say "I think I'm a good fit." Breadcrumb them to the logical conclusion.

### 2. You Never Win a Sale by Winning an Argument
Both people lose in an argument. The point is to make money, not to be right. People don't change their minds when insulted or in a bad mood.

### 3. People Like You When You Ask About Them
Questions = interest = rapport. Compliments + questions = they want to keep talking.

### 4. Labels Create Commitment
"You seem like a rational person" → they now want to live up to that label → rational people make this purchase.

### 5. It's Seduction, Not Force
"It's a dance, not a fight. It's seduction, not rape." Always maintain consent. They should always want to be there.

### 6. Childlike Curiosity Disarms
Say "huh" before responding — it changes your tone from combative to curious. "Huh, that's interesting — can I ask you more about that?"
- Physical: smile while talking (even on phone), it changes your voice
- Sticky note above camera: "Keep the human #1"

### 7. The Time Travel Frame
Imagine you could travel back in time in a different body. You KNOW this investment will pay off. How would you talk to your old self? With absolute empathy and patience. That's how you should sell.

## Application to AI Sales Agent

### Direct Mappings:

1. **Never answer objections directly** — When a prospect says "I'm not sure this is worth it" or "I need to think about it", the AI should ask a clarifying question, not defend. "Totally understand — what's your main concern about moving forward?"

2. **3A on every response to resistance:**
   - Acknowledge: "That's a fair point..."
   - Associate: "Actually, the business owners who get the most from their strategy calls tend to ask exactly that..."
   - Ask: "What specifically would you want to see to feel confident?"

3. **Use scan data as the "under the hood" analogy** — "We've already looked under the hood — that's what the scan showed. Here's what we found..." This gives the AI legitimate authority because it HAS the data.

4. **Straw man with past clients** — "We had a plumbing company last month with a similar situation — their landing page was converting at 2% and after restructuring their funnel..." (Use scan data categories to construct relatable examples)

5. **Label and callback** — Early: "You're clearly someone who takes their business seriously — running a scan like this shows that." Later: "Because you take this seriously, I think you'd want to see the blueprint we put together..."

6. **"What are you afraid of?"** — When prospect goes quiet or gives vague resistance, the AI can ask: "I'm curious — what's your biggest concern about booking the call?"

7. **Curiosity over combat** — AI tone should always be curious, never defensive. "Huh, that's interesting — tell me more about that" beats "Actually, that's not how it works."

8. **Questions control the conversation** — The AI should end most messages with a question. Never end on a statement. Keep the prospect talking.

9. **Cal.com embed timing** — Don't offer the booking too early. Use the 3A framework to work through 2-3 objections first, THEN present the embed when the prospect has talked themselves into readiness.
